Cookbooks let you go beyond a flat list of saved recipes. Instead of scrolling through everything at once, you can sort recipes into named collections that match how you actually cook — by occasion, ingredient type, cooking time, dietary preference, or anything else that makes sense to you. Share the collections you're proud of, or keep your favorites just for yourself.

## How to create a cookbook

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Cookbooks section">
    Go to **Cookbooks** from the main navigation. You'll see any cookbooks you've already created, plus an option to create a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new cookbook">
    Tap **New cookbook** and give it a name and optional description. Pick something specific enough to be useful — "Pasta" is clearer than "Italian."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the privacy level">
    Choose how you want to share this cookbook:
    - **Private** — only you can see it
    - **Friends** — visible to people you follow
    - **Community** — visible to everyone on Foryoueats
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add recipes">
    Open any recipe and tap **Save to cookbook**, then select the cookbook you just created. Or browse your recipe library and add multiple recipes at once from within the cookbook.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Start with a handful of your most-used recipes rather than trying to organize everything at once. You can always add more later.
</Tip>

## Privacy options

Every cookbook has its own privacy setting, and you can change it at any time.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Private" icon="lock">
    Only you can see and access this cookbook. Use this for works-in-progress or recipes you're not ready to share.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Friends" icon="user-check">
    Visible to people you follow on Foryoueats. Good for sharing with family or a close cooking group.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Community" icon="globe">
    Published publicly. Anyone browsing community cookbooks can find, view, and save recipes from it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Changing a cookbook from Community to Private removes it from community browsing immediately. Anyone who previously saved a recipe from it keeps their saved copy.
</Note>

## Sharing cookbooks with friends

When you set a cookbook to **Friends**, the people you follow on Foryoueats can view it from your profile. They can browse the recipes and save any they like to their own recipe library or cookbooks.

<Tip>
  If you want to share a cookbook with someone specific who you don't follow, set it to Community so they can find it, or use the direct link from the cookbook's share menu.
</Tip>

## Browsing community cookbooks

Head to the **Community** section and filter by cookbooks to browse collections published by other home cooks. You can open any cookbook, view its recipes, and save individual recipes to your own library.

See [Community](/features/community) for more on discovering recipes and cookbooks from other cooks.

## Organizing your cookbooks

You can rename or update the description of any cookbook at any time. Reorder recipes within a cookbook by dragging them into the order you prefer — useful if you want the most-used recipes at the top.

<Warning>
  Deleting a cookbook removes the collection permanently. The recipes inside are not deleted — they stay in your recipe library unless you remove them separately.
</Warning>
