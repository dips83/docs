The meal planner gives you a clear, organized view of what you're cooking each day. Pull from recipes you've saved or browse what the community has shared, arrange them across the week, and turn your entire plan into a shopping list in a single action — no copying, no counting, no guesswork.

## What you can do with the meal planner

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Plan by day or week" icon="calendar">
    Lay out meals for each day of the week. Fill in breakfast, lunch, dinner, or snacks at whatever level of detail works for you.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use any recipe" icon="book-open">
    Add your own saved recipes or pick from thousands shared by the community. Mix and match freely.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Generate a shopping list instantly" icon="list">
    Turn your entire week's meal plan into a shopping list with one action. Ingredients are combined automatically.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Available on web and mobile" icon="mobile">
    Access your meal plan from your browser or the Foryoueats mobile app — changes sync across all your devices.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to create your first meal plan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the meal planner">
    Go to the **Meal Planner** section from the main navigation. You'll see a weekly calendar view, starting from the current day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a day and meal slot">
    Tap or click any empty slot (for example, Monday dinner) to open the recipe picker.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a recipe">
    Search your saved recipes or browse community recipes. Select the one you want to add to that slot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat across the week">
    Fill in as many or as few meal slots as you like. You can leave days empty and come back to them later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate your shopping list">
    When your plan is ready, tap **Generate shopping list**. Foryoueats combines all the ingredients from every recipe and creates a shopping list you can use right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You don't need to fill in every slot before generating a shopping list. Generate one whenever your plan feels complete enough, and add to it later as needed.
</Tip>

## Editing and adjusting your plan

You can swap, move, or remove any recipe from your plan at any time. Tap a filled slot to replace the recipe or clear it. If you regenerate a shopping list after making changes, it reflects the updated plan.

<Note>
  The meal planner is fully free. There are no limits on the number of meals you can plan or the number of weeks you can plan ahead.
</Note>

## Using community recipes in your plan

When you pick a recipe for a meal slot, you can search community-shared recipes alongside your own. Community recipes display the author's name so you know who created the dish. Any recipe you add to your plan is used only by you — the original recipe stays unchanged.

<Tip>
  If you find a community recipe you love while planning, save it to one of your cookbooks so you can find it again quickly next time.
</Tip>

## Syncing with shopping lists

The shopping list generated from your meal plan behaves like any other shopping list in Foryoueats. You can add extra items, share it with Pantry Pals, and check off items as you shop. See [Shopping lists](/features/shopping-lists) for details on how shopping lists work.
