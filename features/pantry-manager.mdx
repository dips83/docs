The pantry manager gives you a running inventory of what's in your kitchen. When you're not sure what to cook, it surfaces recipes you can make with ingredients you already have — reducing food waste and cutting down on unnecessary grocery trips. When supplies run low, it works alongside your shopping lists to help you restock efficiently.

## What you can do with the pantry manager

## How to set up your pantry

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Pantry Manager">
    Go to **Pantry** from the main navigation. Your pantry starts empty — you build it up over time as you log what you have.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your ingredients">
    Tap **Add ingredient** and type the name. Optionally add a quantity and unit (for example, "500 g" or "1 can"). You can add as many ingredients as you like.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update as you cook and shop">
    When you use an ingredient, update its quantity or remove it. When you come back from the shops, add your new items. The more current your pantry is, the more useful the recipe matching becomes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find recipes you can make">
    Tap **What can I cook?** to see recipes from your library that match the ingredients in your pantry. Foryoueats shows you the closest matches first.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You don't need to log everything to get value from the pantry manager. Even tracking your most-used staples — olive oil, garlic, pasta, tinned tomatoes — is enough for meaningful recipe matching.
</Tip>

## Finding recipes from your pantry

When you tap **What can I cook?**, Foryoueats compares your pantry against your saved recipes and shows you the best matches. Results are sorted by how many ingredients you already have.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Full matches" icon="circle-check">
    Recipes where you have every ingredient. These appear at the top — you can start cooking straight away.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Near matches" icon="circle-half-stroke">
    Recipes where you have most ingredients but are missing one or two. These show you exactly what's missing so you can decide whether to grab just those items.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Recipe matching works with your saved recipes and cookbooks. Community recipes are not included in pantry matching unless you've saved them to your library.
</Note>

## How the pantry connects to shopping lists

When you generate a shopping list from a recipe or meal plan, Foryoueats checks your pantry automatically. Ingredients you already have in sufficient quantity are flagged, so you don't add them to your list unnecessarily.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a recipe to your shopping list">
    Open a recipe and tap **Add to shopping list**. Foryoueats checks your pantry as it builds the ingredient list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review pantry-matched items">
    Items you already have are shown separately. You can still add them to your list if you want a reminder to double-check, or dismiss them if you're confident you have enough.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add only what you need">
    Confirm the list with just the missing ingredients. Your shopping list stays lean and accurate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Keeping your pantry accurate

The pantry manager is most useful when it reflects what's actually in your kitchen. A few habits help keep it current:

- Update ingredient quantities after you finish cooking.
- Add new items after each grocery trip.
- Remove items when you finish them rather than letting outdated entries accumulate.

<Note>
  The pantry manager is entirely free to use. There's no limit on the number of ingredients you can track.
</Note>
