As your recipe library grows, Foryoueats gives you several ways to keep it organized and find what you're looking for quickly. You can mark favorites, track recipes you've cooked, group recipes into cookbooks, and apply tags — all from the **My Recipes** section.

## My Recipes

**My Recipes** is the main view of your library. It shows every recipe you've saved, imported, or created. Use it as your home base for browsing and managing your collection.

## Mark favorites and track what you've cooked

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Favorites" icon="heart">
    Click the heart icon on any recipe to mark it as a favorite. Filter your library to show only favorites when you want to cook something you already love.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Made It" icon="check">
    Click **Made It** on a recipe after you cook it to track your cooking history. Use this to see which recipes you've actually tried.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Filter and sort your library

Use the filter and sort controls at the top of **My Recipes** to narrow down your options.

**Filter by time:**
- Prep time
- Cook time
- Total time

**Sort by:**
- Newest first
- Oldest first
- Last updated
- Name (A–Z)
- Prep time
- Cook time
- Total time

<Tip>
  Filtering by total time is useful when you have a specific window to cook — for example, finding everything you can make in under 30 minutes.
</Tip>

## Cookbooks

Cookbooks let you group recipes into themed collections. Create a cookbook for a cuisine, a meal type, a season, a diet, or anything else that makes sense for how you cook.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a cookbook">
    In **My Recipes**, click **Cookbooks** in the sidebar, then click **New Cookbook**. Give it a name and optionally a description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add recipes to a cookbook">
    Open any recipe and click **Add to Cookbook**, then select the cookbook you want. You can add one recipe to multiple cookbooks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse a cookbook">
    Click any cookbook name in the sidebar to view only the recipes in that collection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tags and categories

Tags let you label recipes with flexible, freeform keywords — such as *quick*, *vegetarian*, *weeknight*, or *holiday*. Add tags when creating or editing a recipe, then filter your library by tag to find matching recipes.

<Note>
  A recipe can have multiple tags, making it easy to find the right dish from multiple angles — by cuisine, occasion, dietary preference, or cooking method.
</Note>
